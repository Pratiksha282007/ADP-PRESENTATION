Physics Syllabus: LASER, Quantum Physics, Hall Effect, Electromagnetism

LASER (Light Amplification by Stimulated Emission of Radiation)

LASER is a device that produces coherent, monochromatic, and highly directional light through stimulated emission. It involves pumping energy into a medium to create a population inversion, leading to amplification of light waves.

Numerical Example:

Calculate the wavelength of a LASER photon if its energy is 2.5 eV. (Use h = 4.14 × 10^{-15} eV·s, c = 3 × 10^8 m/s)

E = hc / λ ⇒ λ = hc / E = (4.14 × 10^{-15} × 3 × 10^8) / 2.5 ≈ 496 nm.

Quantum Physics

Quantum physics describes the behavior of matter and energy at atomic and subatomic scales. Key concepts include wave-particle duality, quantization of energy, and the uncertainty principle, which states that certain pairs of physical properties cannot be simultaneously known to arbitrary precision.

Numerical Example:

Find the de Broglie wavelength of an electron moving at 2 × 10^6 m/s. (Use m_e = 9.1 × 10^{-31} kg, h = 6.626 × 10^{-34} J·s)

λ = h / p = h / (m v) = 6.626 × 10^{-34} / (9.1 × 10^{-31} × 2 × 10^6) ≈ 3.63 × 10^{-10} m or 0.363 nm.

Hall Effect (Hall Sensor)

The Hall effect is the production of a voltage difference (Hall voltage) across an electrical conductor when a magnetic field is applied perpendicular to the current flow. Hall sensors use this to measure magnetic fields or currents.

Numerical Example:

A copper strip (width w = 1 cm, current I = 10 A) is placed in a magnetic field B = 0.5 T. Calculate the Hall voltage if Hall coefficient R_H = -5.2 × 10^{-11} m^3/C.

V_H = R_H (I B / w) = -5.2 × 10^{-11} × (10 × 0.5 / 0.01) ≈ -2.6 × 10^{-8} V.

Electromagnetism

Electromagnetism deals with the relationship between electric and magnetic fields. Key laws include Faraday's law (induced EMF in a changing magnetic field) and Ampere's law. Maxwell's equations unify these into a complete description of electromagnetic phenomena.

Numerical Example:

A coil with 100 turns and area 0.01 m² has a magnetic field changing at 0.2 T/s. Calculate the induced EMF.

EMF = N dΦ/dt = N A dB/dt = 100 × 0.01 × 0.2 = 0.2 V.
